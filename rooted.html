Rooted
Scroll
A tree, a scroll, a mother

Everything that
reaches began
as something rooted.

A short, interactive story told through a single tree — growing the way she did: quietly, underground, long before anyone could see it.

One

Before there was you,
there was this.

A single point holding everything it would ever need. No leaves yet. No proof of anything. Just enough to begin.

Two

She fed you before she
had a word for it.

Growth doesn't announce itself. It happens in the dark, in the ordinary hours nobody thinks to write down.

Three

Underground, unseen,
she was building what
would hold you up.

Roots don't get applauded. They just keep widening, quietly, so that whatever grows above never has to think about falling.

Four

Every reach you make
was rehearsed first
in her arms.

Balance, distance, direction — all of it practiced somewhere close to her, long before you called it independence.

Five

The wind doesn't ask
permission. Neither
does worry.

She bent. She didn't break. Not because it was easy — because something smaller than her was watching how it's done.

Six

You became visible.
She stayed the same
size — just fuller.

Every bit of colour above ground was paid for by something patient going on beneath it.

Seven

The ones who leave
the nest were never
leaving her.

Just going. A tree doesn't chase what it grew. It opens its hands and calls that love, too.

She never measured love
in how far the branches
travelled — only in staying
rooted enough to let them.
